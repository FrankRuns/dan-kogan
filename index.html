01 — Introduction

There are different
ways to live

I've been in analytics for about 18 years. Target, Wayfair, Lyric, a Substack with 100+ articles, a book I'm finishing. The thread through all of it isn't a discipline or a job title. It's a habit: prototype first, understand second, build third.

WAY 01
Optimize one path.
Pick a lane early. Get really good at one thing. Wait until you understand something fully before touching it. Safe. Predictable. Rarely surprising.
WAY 02
Stay curious, learn fast.
Every new domain is a puzzle. You build small things to understand big things. You're never the expert — you're always the learner who ships. This compounds.
WAY 03
Wait for permission.
Wait for the perfect dataset. Wait for sign-off. Wait until you have a real use case. By the time you're ready, someone else has already learned it the fast way.

"The prototype is not a draft of the thing. It is the process by which you figure out what the thing should be."

I learned more about supply chain network design by building a broken simulation in R on a Saturday afternoon than I did reading three textbooks. The books told me what to think. The broken simulation told me what the problem actually was.

Today we're going to do that together. You'll build intuition by touching the model — not by reading about it.

02 — Lyric & The Buy vs. Build Question

Know what you want
before you build it

Lyric is an AI-first supply chain optimization platform. Clients like Coca-Cola and Google use it to make better network, inventory, and fulfillment decisions. I lead training — we teach people how to use the platform, yes, but more importantly, we teach them how to think about the problem.

Decisions interact. Optimizing one in isolation usually makes another worse.

The hardest part of deploying a platform like Lyric isn't the software. It's that clients often don't know what they actually want to optimize until they're in the middle of it. They think they want lower inventory. They actually want better service levels without holding safety stock in the wrong places. Different problem. Different solution.

// When to Buy
  • Problem is well-understood, solution is known
  • Scale requires reliability you can't build cheaply
  • You need auditability and enterprise integration
  • Your team's time is worth more than vendor cost
  • You want to focus on the decision, not the engine
// When to Prototype First
  • You don't fully understand the problem yet
  • Stakeholders disagree on what success looks like
  • You need to make the invisible visible fast
  • The data is messy and you need to learn its shape
  • You want to enrich the conversation before the build
⬡
I've been prototyping for years — R, Python, Shiny apps, Jupyter notebooks. But with AI, the loop is 10x faster and a lot more fun. I use Claude for upfront brainstorming and exploration: synthetic data generation, quick simulations, visual experiments. The prototype makes my stakeholder conversations richer. By the time we touch the Lyric platform, we know exactly what we're building and why. The prototype earns the conversation.

These two exercises below? I built them with Claude this week — specifically for today. They're not polished products. They're thinking tools. That's the point.

05 — Closing

The prototype earns
the conversation

You will graduate into a world where AI can generate a working simulation in minutes. That changes what's valuable. The skill isn't coding. It's knowing what to build and why. That still requires domain knowledge, good questions, and the discipline to learn fast.

// The habit
Before you deploy anything: prototype it. Before you prototype: understand the tradeoff. Before you understand the tradeoff: ask what the question actually is. Most analytics projects fail at question definition, not execution.
// Buy vs. Build
Platforms like Lyric are powerful when you know what you want. Prototypes are how you figure out what you want. They're not in tension — the prototype is how you earn the right to use the platform well.
// AI changes the loop
I built this entire lecture — widgets included — with Claude over a few days. Not because I couldn't build it alone, but because the loop is 10x faster with a collaborator that never runs out of energy. Learn to use it as a thinking partner, not just a code generator.
// The longer game
Every prototype you build is an artifact that proves you understand something. Build enough of them and you have a portfolio of understanding — which is a much more durable asset than a job title.
