The total cost curve is a U-shape. More warehouses reduce transport cost but add fixed cost. The minimum — usually 3–5 for this network — is the sweet spot. This shape appears everywhere in supply chain: safety stock vs. stockouts, batch size vs. holding cost.
Takeaway → "More is better" is almost never true in network design. Every tradeoff has a minimum. The job of analytics is to find it — and explain why.
