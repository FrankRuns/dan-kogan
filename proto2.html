The total cost curve forms a U-shape. More warehouses reduce transport cost (shorter distances) but add fixed cost. The minimum — usually 3–5 warehouses for this network — is the sweet spot. Push past it and you're paying for buildings faster than you're saving on miles. This U-shape is everywhere in supply chain: safety stock vs. stockouts, batch size vs. holding cost, service level vs. inventory. Recognizing the shape is half the job.
Takeaway → "More is better" is almost never true in network design. Every tradeoff has a minimum. The job of analytics is to find it — and to explain why it's there to someone who didn't run the model.
