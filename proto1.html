The green dot is the demand-weighted centroid — the point that minimizes total cost when cost scales with distance × demand. It pulls toward NYC and LA because they're your heaviest customers. The geographic center is not the cost-optimal center. This is the gravity model intuition: optimal placement follows demand, not geography.
Takeaway → Before you run a network optimization, you need to know your demand distribution. The data requirement follows from understanding the model. Prototyping teaches you what data actually matters.
